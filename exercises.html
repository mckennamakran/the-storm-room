Grounding Exercises

Additional techniques to calm anxiety and find your center

What is Grounding?

Grounding techniques help bring you back to the present moment when anxiety pulls you into worries about the future or regrets about the past. These exercises use your senses to anchor you in the here and now.

5-4-3-2-1 Technique

Engage your senses to bring awareness to the present moment.

  1. 5 things you can see
  2. 4 things you can touch
  3. 3 things you can hear
  4. 2 things you can smell
  5. 1 thing you can taste

Mindful Breathing

Focus on your breath to calm the nervous system.

  1. Sit comfortably with your back straight
  2. Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 counts
  3. Hold your breath for 4 counts
  4. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 counts
  5. Repeat for several cycles

Body Scan Meditation

Bring awareness to each part of your body to release tension.

  1. Lie down or sit comfortably
  2. Start at your toes, noticing any sensations
  3. Slowly move your attention up through your body
  4. Notice each area without judgment
  5. Release tension as you exhale

Color Grounding

Use visual observation to stay present.

  1. Look around your environment
  2. Find 5 things of a specific color (e.g., blue)
  3. Name each object out loud or in your mind
  4. Notice textures, shapes, and details
  5. Repeat with a different color
No timer needed

Temperature Grounding

Use temperature sensations to anchor in the present.

  1. Hold a cold glass of water or an ice cube
  2. Focus on the sensation in your hand
  3. Notice how the temperature feels
  4. Pay attention as it warms in your hand
  5. Alternatively, hold a warm cup of tea
No timer needed

Sound Meditation

Use auditory focus to ground yourself.

  1. Close your eyes and listen to the sounds around you
  2. Identify each distinct sound you hear
  3. Notice the distance, volume, and quality of each sound
  4. Listen without labeling or judging the sounds
  5. Focus on the spaces between sounds
